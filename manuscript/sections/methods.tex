\section{The pipeline, and reproducing Lawson's workflow}\label{sec:methods}

\subsection{Pipeline}
Each contig runs through three stages. RFdiffusion3 generates \num{8} scaffold backbones with the
epitope segment fixed at its contig position (one GPU task per contig, \texttt{n\_batches=1}). Each
backbone is stripped to its $C\alpha$/backbone trace and re-sequenced by ProteinMPNN, which is told to
hold the epitope residues \emph{fixed} --- it designs only the scaffold --- and draws \num{8} sequences
per backbone at temperature \num{0.1}. The epitope's fixed positions are read back from the contig,
since an island sits at residues $N{+}1\ldots N{+}\ell$ of an $N$-flank contig. AlphaFold3 then predicts
each sequence single-sequence (\texttt{--norun\_data\_pipeline}, one diffusion sample, seed~1), giving
the $\num{8}\times\num{8}=\num{64}$ designs per contig that the four filters score against the designed
structure. A few backbones fail to yield sequences in ProteinMPNN for reasons we have not yet diagnosed,
so the realized count falls marginally short of the nominal $\num{5568}\,V$ (for the $V=\num{20}$ run,
\num{111322} of \num{111360}). Generation code lives in \texttt{episcaf\_pipeline/} and
\texttt{scripts/}; metrics, scoring, and visualization in \texttt{episcaf\_analysis/}.

\paragraph{Benchmarking.} Rough per-step throughput, to size cluster wall-time requests:
RFdiffusion3 produces a contig's \num{8} backbones in under \SI{10}{\minute} on one A100;
ProteinMPNN sequences $\sim\!\num{215}$ backbones/hour ($\approx\SI{17}{\second}$ for a
backbone's \num{8} sequences); AlphaFold3 single-sequence predictions are fast but unmeasured
on our V100 nodes~[verify]. A run's wall-time then follows by multiplication --- e.g.\ a
\num{300}-backbone ProteinMPNN batch is $\approx\SI{1.4}{\hour}$, for which we request
\SI{8}{\hour} to leave margin.

\subsection{Reproducing Lawson's baseline}\label{sec:repro}
Before trusting any new number, verify the metrics against ground truth. \texttt{compute\_metrics.py
--validate} reads \texttt{dp2.parquet} (Lawson's stored per-design values), recomputes
\texttt{overall\_rmsd} and \texttt{epitope\_chunk\_rmsd} from his ProteinMPNN PDBs and AlphaFold3 CIFs,
and diffs them against the stored values. Agreement here is the precondition for everything downstream:
it confirms our four-filter implementation (Section~\ref{sec:fourfilter}) matches the workflow we are
extending, so a later difference reflects the design protocol, not a scoring bug.
