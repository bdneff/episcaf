\subsection{12-mer epitopes pass more often than DP3 epitopes}
Using epitope-chunk RMSD $< \SI{2.5}{\angstrom}$ as a common bar, the tiled 12-mer
antigens all pass more often than the pooled DP3 / antibody set:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
\toprule
set & pass rate & median epitope RMSD (\si{\angstrom}) \\
\midrule
DP3 / mAb (pooled) & 30.0\% & 5.0 \\
6M0J               & 47.0\% & 2.65 \\
1D2K               & 60.2\% & 1.85 \\
4WAT               & 84.4\% & 0.43 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\noindent
Even the hardest 12-mer antigen (6M0J) clears the bar more often than DP3. Re-running the
scorer's gate over the full 12-mer set reproduces this: \num{54539} of \num{82712} designs
pass ($\approx 65.9\%$ pooled), consistent with the per-antigen rates above. The full
distribution comparison is in Fig.~\ref{fig:passrates}.

\paragraph{Caveats (important for interpretation).}
The 12-mer epitope is a single contiguous 12-residue stretch, whereas DP3 epitopes are
larger and often multi-island, so 12-mer RMSD/PAE are mechanically lower in part by
\emph{definition}; the pooled DP3 30\% also averages over heterogeneous epitopes. The
clean control is to stratify \emph{within} DP3 by island count/size
(Section~\ref{sec:open}). Cylinder counts are also not comparable across sets: 12-mer
cylinder medians (\textasciitilde31--32) exceed DP3 (\textasciitilde21) mainly because the
longer scaffolds place more C$\alpha$ atoms in a fixed cylinder --- a scaffold-size
artifact, not more clashing. As a sanity check, the DP3 cylinder median ($\approx 21$)
tracks its real \texttt{af3\_n\_clash\_res} median ($\approx 19$).

\subsection{The native-aware carve removes false-positive penalties}
Among truly clash-free designs, excluding scaffold residues that sit in native-occupied
space lowers the cylinder count and, in particular, cuts the heavily-penalized
high-count tail, while barely changing genuinely clashing designs. The communication
framing for the result: \emph{\textasciitilde730 designs the antibody accepts were being
penalized for residues sitting where the native antigen already sits --- they no longer
are.} See Fig.~\ref{fig:fp}.
